# ProposaIQ Marketing Launch Pack

## Hard truth

- Do not market this as "an AI proposal generator." That is crowded, generic, and forgettable.
- Your only defensible angle is trust: vague client briefs force freelancers to bluff on scope, timeline, and costs. ProposaIQ slows that down and makes the uncertainty visible.
- Reddit and X are not dependable acquisition channels for this product. They can create feedback loops and a few early users. They will not save a weak position.
- Product Hunt is a bad use of attention right now. You do not have testimonials, analytics, or a strong enough social proof loop yet.

## Positioning that might work

- ICP: freelance developers and very small software agencies writing custom project proposals.
- Pain: client briefs are vague, proposals get padded with guesses, and better-prepared competitors win.
- Core claim: ProposaIQ asks clarification questions before drafting, separates cited facts from estimates, and exposes assumptions instead of hiding them.
- What to stop saying: "write proposals in seconds", "AI writer", "save hours" without proof.

## Channel priority

1. LinkedIn
   Best chance of reaching freelancers under their professional identity. Use it for demos, before/after examples, and direct outreach.
2. Reddit
   Good for pain discovery and comment-based distribution. Bad for lazy link drops.
3. X
   Fine for repeated visibility. Weak for direct conversion unless you already have an audience.
4. Indie Hackers
   Good for founder feedback and a small amount of traffic. Mostly peers, not customers.
5. Hacker News
   Only use if the live product is usable without signup and you are ready to answer technical questions in public.

## What will fail

- Posting the same launch blurb everywhere.
- Leading with "I built..." instead of the freelancer pain.
- Sending people to a landing page preview that has no strong social card.
- Treating founder communities as proof of demand from freelancers.
- Marketing broad "proposal writing" instead of "software freelancers dealing with vague briefs."

## Reply bank

### "Why not just use ChatGPT?"

```text
Because generic generation is exactly the problem. The point here is not writing fluency. It is controlling uncertainty, asking for missing detail, and separating facts from guesses before the draft gets sent.
```

### "Who is this really for?"

```text
Freelance developers and small dev agencies writing proposals for custom software work. It is not meant for generic copywriting or RFP-heavy enterprise sales teams.
```

### "What proof do you have this matters?"

```text
The honest answer is early-stage proof, not strong proof. The pain is real. The market proof still has to come from repeated usage and whether freelancers trust the output enough to use it on live deals.
```

## What to measure

- Number of people who paste a real brief
- Number of clarification flows completed
- Number of full proposals generated
- Number of returning users by channel
- Number of replies that mention trust, vague briefs, or bluffing

## Verdict

PIVOT your marketing away from "AI proposal generator" and toward "trust-first proposal drafting for freelance developers dealing with vague briefs."
